# [SECTION 1 — PROBLEM]

## Slide 2 — Section Header: The Problem

**Tag:** `organizational-knowledge-loss | sme-challenges`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Let me paint a picture of what's actually happening inside SMEs today."

---

## Slide 3 — Pain Point 1: Knowledge Fragmentation

**Tag:** `meeting-notes-lost | incomplete-documentation`

**Speaker Script:**
> "First problem: knowledge fragmentation. In most organizations, employees are expected to simultaneously participate in discussions while taking notes, tracking action items, and remembering key decisions. The result? Incomplete records. Inconsistent documentation. Missing context.
>
> And the recordings that do exist? They're rarely revisited. Because reviewing a one-hour meeting takes thirty to sixty minutes manually. People don't have that time."

---

## Slide 4 — Pain Point 2: Knowledge Loss on Employee Turnover

**Tag:** `institutional-knowledge-lost | employee-turnover`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Second problem: when employees resign or teams change, historical decisions, project reasoning, and technical discussions disappear with them. We've all seen it happen. New employees start from scratch. The same topics get re-discussed. Projects lose continuity.
>
> This is a structural problem, not a people problem. It's the cost of treating meetings as isolated events instead of connected business contexts."

---

## Slide 5 — Pain Point 3: Privacy Concerns with Cloud AI Tools

**Tag:** `cloud-privacy-risk | data-compliance-concerns`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Third problem: the AI tools that exist to help — they actually make things worse in one critical way. Most transcription platforms are cloud-first. Your sensitive business discussions get uploaded to third-party servers. Financial planning, client conversations, strategic decisions — all sitting on someone else's infrastructure.
>
> SMEs handling confidential data are right to be hesitant. The privacy concern alone is enough to block adoption entirely."

---

## Slide 6 — Pain Point 4: Subscription Fragmentation

**Tag:** `subscription-fatigue | tool-overload`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Fourth problem: the productivity ecosystem is broken. Organizations pay separately for transcription tools, note-taking apps, collaboration software, and cloud storage — and still don't have a unified, searchable organizational memory.
>
> We're paying more and getting less coherence. Less continuity. Less actual institutional intelligence."

# [SECTION 3 — SOLUTION]

## Slide 9 — Section Header: The Solution

**Tag:** `remembry-introduction | privacy-first-desktop-app`

**Speaker Script:**
> "So what is Remembry? Let me be direct.
>
> Remembry is a privacy-first desktop application that transforms meeting recordings into structured, searchable, long-term organizational knowledge — using multi-modal AI.
>
> Not just transcription. Not just summarization. Actual memory that stays, is searchable, and serves the organization over time."

---

## Slide 10 — Solution Overview: What Remembry Does

**Tag:** `ai-powered-transcription | structured-knowledge-extraction`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Here's how it works. Users upload or record meeting audio directly inside the application. Our AI pipeline — powered by Gemini — handles the rest.
>
> It doesn't just transcribe. It extracts structured outputs: summaries, action items, decisions made, Q&A records, key discussion points, technical insights, and semantic concepts. All stored locally in SQLite. All under the organization's control.
>
> Users bring their own Gemini API key. No vendor lock-in. No subscription barrier. Low cost, high control."

---

## Slide 11 — Core Feature: Local-First Privacy Architecture

**Tag:** `local-storage | data-ownership | compliance-ready`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Let's talk about the architectural difference. Remembry is local-first. Everything — transcripts, extracted notes, meeting records — stays on the user's device. Nothing goes to external servers permanently.
>
> This means: full privacy. Full data ownership. Compliance readiness for regulated industries. And fundamentally — organizational trust. Your sensitive discussions never leave your control."

---

## Slide 12 — Core Feature: Semantic Search and Q&A

**Tag:** `searchable-memory | contextual-retrieval | ai-query`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Second core capability: semantic search and contextual retrieval. This is where Remembry moves beyond a transcription tool into an actual memory system.
>
> Users can ask questions like — 'Why was Project Alpha postponed?' 'What recurring issues were mentioned by clients this quarter?' 'Which technical decisions were made in Q3?'
>
> Remembry retrieves the relevant discussions, decisions, and evidence. Not just keywords. Actual contextual memory."

---

## Slide 13 — Core Feature: Project-Based Organization

**Tag:** `project-workspaces | meeting-continuity | knowledge-traceability`

**Speaker Script:**
> "Third capability: project-based knowledge organization. Meetings and transcripts are grouped into projects and workspaces. This maintains continuity across long-term initiatives.
>
> Instead of isolated meeting notes, you get an organized institutional record — searchable by project, by topic, by time period, by decision type. Information fragmentation drops significantly. Traceability improves."

> Documentation consistency: improved by approximately seventy percent. Structured extraction replaces variable manual note-taking.

> Post-meeting administrative workload: reduced by approximately seventy to eighty percent. AI handles the summarization work.

>
> The cloud-first approach that dominated the first wave of AI meeting tools is increasingly seen as a liability. Organizations — especially SMEs handling sensitive discussions — want control. They want data ownership. They want compliance-ready infrastructure.
>
> Remembry is positioned directly at this demand shift."
